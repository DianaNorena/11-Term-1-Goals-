Our Big Goal: Communicating with Confidence

This year in 11th-grade English, our core objective is to empower you to become a confident and effective communicator in all four language skills: reading, listening, speaking, and writing. We'll work together to hone your ability to understand and express both explicit and implicit information, ideas, and arguments. By building proficiency in a wide array of language structures, vocabulary, and linking devices, you'll be well-prepared to not only ace your IGCSE exam but also lay a robust groundwork for future academic endeavors, such as the IELTS. You've got this!

Social-Emotional Learning (SEL) Goals This Term

Beyond English skills, we'll also focus on developing key social-emotional competencies that are crucial for your success both in and out of school.

  • Self-Awareness & Self-Management: I recognize my strengths and weaknesses in order to prepare myself for life after school.
  • Relationship Skills: I am open to the suggestions of others and I am willing to incorporate their ideas.
  • Responsible Decision-Making: I understand the meaning of "every moment counts" as it applies to time management and activity planning for school life.

Non-Cambridge Assessment Guidelines

Reading and Listening Assessments

For our non-Cambridge assessments in Reading and Listening, you must demonstrate competencies across all proficiency levels.

  • Meeting is a prerequisite to achieve the Exceeding level.
  • Meeting and Exceeding are prerequisites to achieve the Excelling level.

Following Cambridge guidelines, you must get at least 70% of the answers correct to be awarded any level.

Open-ended questions are evaluated using a rubric where:

  • Content accounts for 40%
  • Evidence provided accounts for 35%
  • Use of English accounts for 25%

Speaking and Writing Assessments

Speaking and Writing will be assessed using adapted versions of Cambridge Rubrics, ensuring consistency and clear criteria for your performance.
